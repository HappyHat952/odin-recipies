Description

This Western-style dish is unique to Japan, as it doesnâ€™t exist in American or European cuisine. Several restaurants in Japan claim to have created omurice in the early 1900s. However, it was in 1925 that a chef in Osaka at a Western-style diner called Hokkyokusei created a dish very similar to the omurice that we know and love today.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 onion
  • chicken
  • 1.75 cups day old rice
  • 5 tbsp ketchup
  • 4 large eggs
  • salt

Steps

  1. Cook the onions and chicken on a pan
  2. When onions and chicken are cooked fully, add the day old rice
  3. Stir fry the pan for 5 minutes, then add ketchup to the rice
  4. Form a lump of rice on the plate
  5. Prepare the omlette, making sure to use chopsticks to scrable on top
  6. Lay the omlette on top of the rice
  7. Add decorations
